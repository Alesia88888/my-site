PMBOK Body of Knowledge to know

The Project Management Body of Knowledge is a set of guidelines helping in managing a project. The PMBOK knowledge is evolving all the time and due to this published standards are being updated as well. Right now the most current PMBOK version is the 6th one. It is important to pay attention to all 10 areas of knowledge to keep everything under control. PMBOK establishes detailed methodology with steps on how project manager shall manage all of these aspects.

10 PMBOK Body of Knowledge:

  1. Integration Management is about the processes in a project that helps to establish order of action and organize the whole process of work. It is also described how to prepare a project charter and project plan.
  2. Scope Management is about managing work and deliverables of a project and preparing such documentation as scope statement and work breakdown structure.
  3. Time Management is about the process of time management, while project is defined by a limited period of time and how to make a plan schedule and control it efficiently.
  4. Cost Management is about determining a method to estimate a budget and cost controlling.
  5. Quality Management a project need to be delivered in estimated time and budget, but if quality requirements are not set and not monitored, project will not be successful. Quality management is a part of project plan, however while itâ€™s value is really significant, a separate document might be established for proper monitoring.
  6. Human Resource Management is about organizing and successful coordination of a project team.
  7. Communications Management is communications is a vital part of a project which needs its planning and timely execution. Well established processes in this field will bring significant benefit to the project.
  8. Project Risk Management is about types of risks on a project, processes of collecting, estimating and monitoring risks and documentation which will help.
  9. Procurement Management is about organizing a process of acquiring external resources needed for a project, selecting vendors and preparing contracts.
  10. Stakeholder Management is about identification of all parties interested in a project, their prioritization and gathering requirements and feedback from them to ensure that the project result will satisfy their needs.

Without good processes projects might be chaotic and ambiguous, PMBOK knowledge will help you to organize everything from inception and keep everything under control.
