What is a project manager?

Project manager is a motivated and enthusiastic individual playing a leading role in successful realization of a project with overall responsibility for initiation, planning, monitoring, and execution of a project. Project managers are accountable for project scope, resources and communication, change management on a project, as well as risk control and minimizing of uncertainty.

Although they do not fulfill all the work themselves, they still responsible for the required work being completed in time. They perform risk analysis and monitor on different stages of a project to identify and assess factors that could negatively affect the success of a project. A big part of project managers’ job is associated with communication. Facilitation of open communication ensures that everyone can express their opinion, decreases level of risk and improves collaboration with stakeholders and within a team.

Project managers select a peroject methodology establish processes. They periodically prepare reports to keep track on what is done, what is planned to be done and whether project stays within a schedule. It their responsibilitu as well to ensure that everyone understand their roles and responsibilities on a project.

What are responsibilities of a Project Manager?

    Initiation:
  1. selection of project methodology;
  2. identifying stakeholders;
  3. preparation of project documentation templates and project charter.
    Planning:
  1. requirements analysis;
  2. risk analysis;
  3. determine time, budget, scope and resources needed for a project;
  4. project plan preparation;
  5. work breakdown structure.
    Execution:
  1. monitoring and controlling work against plan and schedule;
  2. communication management;
  3. risk monitoring;
  4. change management;
  5. remove obstacles and motivates team members;
  6. communicate project status to the customers;
  7. boost stakeholders involvement.
    Delivery:
  1. organization of software product transfer to the customer;
  2. prepare necessary documentation for project closure;
  3. receiving feedback from a customer;
  4. organizing retrospective meeting for a team;
  5. add lessons learned to knowledge base for future projects.

As we’ve seen Project Manager is a really significant person on a project who shall be goal-oriented, organized, comfortable with changing in a working environment, able and willing to solve various problems and make team members and stakeholders happy. If this all describes you, then you shall definitely consider this kind of job.
